##  Copy a Chart or Graph From Excel

 The copy process for [a chart in Excel](https://technical-tips.techidaily.com/top-techniques-for-enhancing-photo-quality-on-your-ios-device/) isn't anything unusual. Just be sure you select the chart itself and not an individual element on the chart.

 With the chart selected, do one of the following:

* Press Ctrl+C on Windows or Command+C on Mac.
* Go to the Home tab and click "Copy" on the left side of the ribbon.
* [Right-click](https://desktop-recording.techidaily.com/updated-the-art-of-recording-fun-6-techniques-to-document-minecraft-for-2024/) and pick "Copy."

![Select Copy](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2021/11/Copy-ExcelCopyPasteChart.png) 

 All of these copy actions place the chart on [your clipboard](https://change-location.techidaily.com/in-2024-3-ways-for-android-pokemon-go-spoofing-on-vivo-y27-4g-drfone-by-drfone-virtual-android/). It's then ready for you to paste where needed.

##  Paste a Chart in an Office Application

Related: [How to Link or Embed an Excel Worksheet in a Word Document](https://extra-approaches.techidaily.com/new-reviewing-the-unseen-facebooks-privacy-and-security-guide/) 

 You can easily paste an Excel chart [into another Microsoft Office application such as Word](https://extra-approaches.techidaily.com/new-reviewing-the-unseen-facebooks-privacy-and-security-guide/), PowerPoint, or Outlook. As mentioned, you can paste the chart as an image, embed it, or paste it with a link to the source workbook. And you can choose one of these options before or after you paste the chart.

 With this first method, you'll paste the chart and then choose the Paste Special option. Place your cursor in the application where you want to paste the chart and do one of the following:

* Press Ctrl+V on Windows or Command+V on Mac.
* Go to the Home tab (Word and PowerPoint) or the Message tab (Outlook) and click "Paste" on the left side of the ribbon.

 Then, click the [Paste Special](https://extra-support.techidaily.com/2024-approved-step-by-step-perfectly-sharing-your-photography-on-youtube/) button that appears at the bottom of the chart. You'll see your options.

###  Paste Special Options

 Here are the different paste special options you can pick from and what they mean.

* **Use Destination Theme & Embed Workbook**: Paste the chart and [match the formatting](https://activate-lock.techidaily.com/how-to-bypass-icloud-lock-on-apple-iphone-12-pro-by-drfone-ios/) or theme in the application where you're pasting. There is no connected link to automatically update the chart if you change the data in Excel.
* **Keep Source Formatting & Embed Workbook**: Paste the chart and keep the formatting or [theme from the source spreadsheet in Excel](https://snapchat-videos.techidaily.com/new-djd-snaps-adding-tracks-to-enhance-videos-for-2024/). There is no connected link to automatically update the chart.
* **Use Destination Theme & Link Data**: Paste the chart, match the formatting or theme, and link to the data in Excel. If you change the spreadsheet, the chart will automatically update in the destination application.
* **Keep Source Formatting & Link Data**: Paste the chart, keep the formatting or theme from the source, and link to the data in Excel. If you change the spreadsheet, the chart will automatically update.
* **Picture**: Paste the chart as an image. There is no link to the data in Excel and any edits you make are as a picture only. The chart is treated as any other image in the application.

 You can also paste the chart as an [object or specific image](https://fox-boxes.techidaily.com/simplified-steps-for-photo-motion-blur-in-photoshop/) format. Go to the Home or Message tab, click the Paste drop-down arrow, and select "Paste Special."
