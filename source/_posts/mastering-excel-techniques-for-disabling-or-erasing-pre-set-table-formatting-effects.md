##  Clear a Table's Formatting in Excel

 To begin the table format removal process, open your spreadsheet with Microsoft Excel.

 In your spreadsheet, click any cell of your table.

![Select a cell of a table.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/1-select-table-cell.png) 

 At the top, in [Excel's ribbon](https://screen-mirror.techidaily.com/in-2024-how-to-mirror-honor-play-40c-to-mac-drfone-by-drfone-android/), click the "Table Design" tab.

![Click the "Table Design" tab in Excel.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/2-excel-table-design-tab.png) 

 On the "Table Design" tab, in the "Table Styles" section, click the "More" option (a down-arrow icon with a line at the top of it).

![Click the "More" option in the "Table Design" tab.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/3-table-styles-more-menu.png) 

 In the "More" menu that opens, click "Clear."

![Select "Clear" from the "More" menu.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/4-remove-table-formatting-excel.png) 

 You've successfully removed your table's formatting.

 To quickly restore your table's formatting, press Ctrl+Z (Windows) or Command+Z (Mac).

![An unformatted table in Excel.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/5-table-formatting-cleared-excel.png) 

 And that's how you turn your stylized tables into plain regular tables in Microsoft Excel!
